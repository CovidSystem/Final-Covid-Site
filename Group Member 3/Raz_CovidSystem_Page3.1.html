Pandemic Protocol

What happens after a disease is declared a pandemic?


Phase I: Preparation and Situation Analysis

In the preparation and analysis phase, a multisectoral pandemic preparedness planning committee must be established.

- Determine the organization structure and leadership roles of said committee, and then raise awareness of its group members for the public to stay informed.
- Calculate budgets and get approval from appropriate authorities.



PHASE II: Developing or updating a plan

Hold a stakeholder consultation to finalize the draft of the national pandemic preparedness plan. Stakeholders may include:
- Relevant national government agencies
- Representatives of different levels of government
- Community leaders
- International organizations
- NGOs
- Relevant professional organizations
- Others identified by the committee

The lead writer then revises the plan based on the outcomes of the consultation.



PHASE III: Evaluating, finalizing & disseminating the plan

Now the pandemic preparedness plan is ready to go into the final stages:

- A simulation exercise to test the effectiveness of the plan is evaluated
- Gaps or final areas for revision of the plan are identified.
- Pandemic preparedness plan is revised and finalized.
- Approval of the plan by appropriate authorities can now be obtained.
- Official and finalized plan is released to stakeholders and the public.
