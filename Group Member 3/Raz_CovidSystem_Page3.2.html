Pandemic System
State and Federal Government Responsibilities

Now that we have established an Emergency Protocol ,
The Government on both Federal and State levels work to inform the public


Federal Responsibilities

The federal government is in charge of the following:

- Interstate travel

- Virus-spread risks (Example: the CDC has the power to invoke quarantine)

- Coordinating supplies such as personal protective equipment and ventilators to ensure that states are not competing for these supplies.



State Responsibilities


States have more power within stat-at-home orders and business closures.

- States can also serve as "lab rats" for trying out what does and doesn't work abut the pandemic preparedness plan on the large scale.

- States also have the authorities to public health emergency and police powers.



Where They Clash

One of the biggest clashes between federal and state government that is expressed during a pandemic has to do with response infrastructure and public health surveillance.

- While the federal government is responsible for devising a plan and surveilling the overall changes in public health as the plan proves effective or ineffective,
- Actual decisions about what remains open or closed is decided at the state level.
- This has proved to be a problem in our current pandemic, states such as North and South Dakota, Wyoming, Iowa, Utah, and Nebraska had no Stay-at-Home orders as last as mid April.
- Their governments rely on voluntary compliance with the CDC's orders
