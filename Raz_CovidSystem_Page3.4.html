What Does Everyday Life During a Pandemic Look Like?


Essential Workers

Essential workers resume work under new circumstances.

- Personal protective equipment is distributed - With nonessential people out of the way, there are less people to work with. - The figure to the right shows the breakdown of essential workers by department.



Nonessential Workers, the Unemployed, and Students

Many nonessential workers have been able to work remotely from home, in order to adhere to state closure laws.
Meanwhile, the federal government issued unemployment check to those whose jobs are not essential and cannot be done remotely.
Lastly, students and those who do not qualify for unemployment spend their time during the pandemic trying to stay as busy as possible within their homes.
Activities include but are not limited to: watching Netflix, reading, baking a lot of bread for some reason, and a lot of social media.
